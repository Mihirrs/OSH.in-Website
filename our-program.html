OSH is a personalised online homeschooling program for students from Grade 1 to Grade 12. Every student has a dedicated teacher who monitors their progress and adapts the content around what that specific child actually needs — not a fixed course pushed at the same pace to everyone. The curriculum draws on Cambridge and California Science frameworks for early grades and aligns fully with the NIOS syllabus for Class 10 and 12. Everything is delivered online, accessed through the OSH student portal, and available when your child is ready — not at a scheduled time.

What Makes OSH Different

🎯

Content That Adapts to the Student

Most online learning platforms deliver the same content in the same order to every student regardless of where they are starting from. OSH does not. A dedicated teacher continuously adjusts each student’s path based on their foundations, their pace, and their goals. Two students in the same grade will move through OSH differently because they are different children.

👥

A Real Teacher — Not a Help Desk

Every OSH student has one specific teacher responsible for their progress. Not a shared inbox. Not a support ticket system. A person who knows your child’s work, tracks how they are developing, and is available through the platform whenever a question comes up. The parent does not have to be the subject expert. That responsibility sits with the teacher.

🏫

Built by Someone Who Went Through This

OSH was founded by someone who was homeschooled their entire life in Bangalore, completed Class 10 and Class 12 through NIOS, and went on to IIT Madras. The program is not built on theory — it is built on direct experience of what works and what does not, from the inside of the process this program is designed to support.

I was homeschooled my entire life and did Class 10 and 12 through NIOS. When I was going through it, the thing that made the real difference was having people around me who had actually been through the same path — not advisors, not administrators, just people who knew what it felt like. Most families I meet don’t have that. That is the only reason OSH exists.

— Mihir  ·  More about the team →
